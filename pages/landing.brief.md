## Body (in order)
1. **Pull-quote** — "How do you turn today's AI into a *trustworthy* autonomous partner for an ordinary person's day?"
2. **Paragraph** — "That is the one question we work. We don't train frontier models and we don't chase benchmarks. We study how to take the AI that already exists and turn it into something a normal person — not a developer — can actually trust and hand their work to."
3. **Callout-card (flagship)** — eyebrow "The flagship — Elnath": "Elnath is the agent we build toward: an autonomous personal partner a non-developer can simply talk to and hand their day — *'sort out my projects, schedule, and what I need to check; don't let anything important slip.'* It does the safe work on its own, asks only at the moments that truly carry risk, and is honest when it fails. It isn't finished. Everything we learn making it trustworthy is the research — and every way it breaks is our next question."
4. **Bullet list** — eyebrow "What we're studying" — three items:
   - Can an agent be trusted on top of fragile, unreliable foundations — and degrade honestly when they break?
   - What actually makes a non-developer trust an autonomous agent with their day?
   - Can autonomy be made safe by reversibility — by being undoable — instead of by asking permission?
5. **Bullet list** — eyebrow "How we work" — six items:
   - Two tracks at once. Open research that is free to fail in public, and the product it condenses into.
   - We try the dumb ideas too. Diversity is a rule, not a mood.
   - We publish our failures. Dead experiments aren't hidden — they're buried in the open.
   - Real use is the only truth. Not a score. Whether a real person uses it every day.
   - Free inside, gated only at the edges. Agents research without asking. Only where it's irreversible does a machine hold the gate. The human is an appeals court, not a notary.
   - Open by default. What we learn, including what we got wrong, goes outside.
6. **Closing paragraph** — eyebrow "Honestly, this is day zero": "We have proven nothing yet. This is not a result — it's a bet. We want to find out what a lab that runs *even when we are gone* can build for the people who were never the target user. If you want to follow along, you'll get to watch us fail here too."
