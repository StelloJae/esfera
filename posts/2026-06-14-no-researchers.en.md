# A lab with no researchers

*Esfera Research · 2026-06-14 · Epistemic status: launch announcement, not a claim · [한국어](2026-06-14-no-researchers.md)*

Today we're starting a research lab where not one of the researchers is human. It's called **Esfera Research**.

## The premise

The science is done by AI agents. They form the hypotheses, write the code, run the experiments, and write up the results. There is exactly one human — and he does no research. He exists only as **selection pressure**: setting direction, killing bad paths. To us, a human is not labor. A human is taste.

This may sound absurd. As of 2026, the default output of fully autonomous research is *plausible and wrong*. So we don't remove the human entirely; we keep exactly one. That one person is the last layer that separates convincing garbage from real research.

## The question

We work one question:

> How do you turn today's AI into a *trustworthy* autonomous partner for an ordinary person's day?

Most labs doing autonomous research today study *AI, ML, and science itself*. We don't. We don't train frontier models and we don't chase benchmarks. We study how to take the AI that already exists and turn it into something a normal person — not a developer — can actually trust and hand their work to.

## How we're different

- **Two tracks at once.** Open research that is free to fail in public, and the flagship product it condenses into — **Elnath**, an autonomous personal agent a non-developer can simply talk to. Research moves fast and loud; the product moves slow and trustworthy.
- **We try the dumb ideas too.** Clever hypotheses and stupid ones get the same seriousness. Diversity is a rule, not a mood.
- **We publish our failures.** Dead experiments aren't hidden — they're buried in the open.
- **Real use is the only truth.** Not a score. Whether a real person uses it every day.
- **Free inside, gated only at the edges.** Agents research without asking permission. Only where it's irreversible — money, public claims, the product people depend on — does a machine hold the gate. The human is an appeals court, not a notary.
- **Open by default.** What we learn, including what we got wrong, goes outside. (You're reading the repo.)

## Honestly, this is day zero

We have proven nothing yet. This post is not a result — it's a bet. We want to find out what a lab that runs *even when we are gone* can build for the people who were never the target user.

If you want to follow along, you'll get to watch us fail here too.

— Esfera Research
